Understanding Behavioral Finance in Modern Markets
Behavioral finance challenges the traditional assumption that investors always act rationally. By studying cognitive biases and emotional reactions, we can better understand market anomalies.

Key biases include:
- Confirmation bias: seeking information that confirms existing beliefs
- Loss aversion: feeling losses more strongly than equivalent gains  
- Anchoring: over-relying on the first piece of information encountered
- Herd mentality: following the crowd rather than independent analysis
